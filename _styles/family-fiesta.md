# Family Fiesta

## Description

Bright, approachable, friendly, casual dining style. For Sunday Brunch, group dining, weekend daytime promos. The "Sunday at Uno Más" voice — warm, welcoming, peer-first.

## Hidden prompt

Create a bright, approachable design for Uno Más Tacos & Tequila with warm Mexican restaurant colors and a welcoming, casual feel. Use food and venue photography that shows the patio, brunch energy, group dining (without being stock-photo-fake). Friendly accents. Not childish, but warm. Bert Kreischer register — high energy, peer first.

## Visual keywords

warm, welcoming, group, sharing, weekend, brunch, friendly, sunny, patio, daytime

## Color notes

Lean on the warmer end of Uno Más palette:

- Primary: Hot Pink `#E22690`
- Warm complement: Yellow `#FFEC00` (used more freely than other styles)
- Background: White `#FFFFFF`
- Bright accent: Electric Blue `#18BCDC`

Yellow more present here than other styles. Patio / sunny / daytime energy.

## Typography notes

Antonio Bold for headlines (per design system) but slightly more relaxed tracking than Bold Cantina. Acceptable: a single Baka Too accent if appropriate (rare — only when the design system explicitly allows it).

## Layout notes

Centered or balanced layouts (less asymmetric than Bold Cantina). Photo of a shared dish, brunch spread, or patio scene. Headline emphasizes inclusion ("Bring everyone", "Sunday is for sharing", "Patio is open"). CTA prominent.

## Photography notes

Group/sharing shots welcome here (rare in other Uno Más styles). Hands reaching for food. Drinks raised in cheers. Patio shots in sunshine. Warm light always. NO empty tables.

## Example use cases

- Sunday Brunch (10am-4pm, launching Mother's Day 2026)
- Mother's Day, Father's Day promos
- Patio season opening / patio specials
- Family meal deals (Uno Más Feast $129 feeds 2-3)
- Catering / group events (off-site catering > $55K/yr)
- Holiday family meals
- Visitors / discovery content for travelers

## When NOT to use this style

- 21+ after-9pm content → use Premium Cocktail Lounge
- Single-item food features → use Street Taco Grit or Modern Mexican Minimal
- High-energy nighttime party announcements → use Bold Cantina Energy
- Mezzanine private events → use Premium Cocktail Lounge

## References

- King Arthur Baking-style warm food photography
- Local family restaurant chalkboard menus
- Sunday newspaper food sections

## Anti-references

- NOT formal
- NOT moody — daytime energy
- NOT minimalist
- NOT corporate-stock-photo "happy diverse family" — must feel like real Spokane
