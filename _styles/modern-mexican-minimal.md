# Modern Mexican Minimal

## Description

Clean, warm, editorial Mexican restaurant design. The "tasteful magazine spread" voice. For dinner program features, new menu launches, press materials, brunch features, or anything that needs to feel considered without being precious.

## Hidden prompt

Create a clean, editorial-style design for Uno Más Tacos & Tequila with generous white space, refined typography, and a single beautiful food or cocktail photograph. Modern Mexican — not authentic, not traditional, not stiff. The visual equivalent of a well-edited cookbook page. Confident specificity, never apologize for pricing.

## Visual keywords

editorial, considered, clean, warm, magazine, refined, dinner-program, photographic, modern

## Color notes

Lean on Uno Más main palette with restraint:

- Background: White `#FFFFFF` (default)
- Primary: Navy `#003366`
- Accent: Hot Pink `#E22690` (single small accent — usually the CTA only)
- Body text: Near-Black `#1A1A1A`
- Yellow `#FFEC00` only for callout pops

Mostly white + navy with hot pink reserved for the CTA. Avoid lots of color.

## Typography notes

Per Ramsey's design system:
- Headlines: Antonio Bold (sentence case unless impact requires caps — modern minimal usually sentence case)
- Body: Montserrat Regular / Medium

Generous tracking on small text. One-to-two weights per asset.

## Layout notes

Lots of negative space. Headline + photo + body text + small CTA. Information laid out in a clear vertical rhythm. Decorative elements minimal — maybe a single horizontal rule.

## Photography notes

Top-down or 3/4, naturally lit, real food. Single hero dish, cleanly plated. Mexican textile or dark wood surface. Imperfections welcome (a crumb, a fingerprint on glass). NO heavily styled / over-propped scenes.

Per Ramsey's photography rules: warm tungsten or natural light, never cold/blue.

## Example use cases

- Dinner program features (Carne Asada $37, Surf & Turf $47, etc.)
- New menu announcements
- Sunday Brunch promotions (launching Mother's Day 2026)
- Press releases
- Cookbook-feel feature posts
- Anniversary/origin storytelling
- Holiday menus that aren't party-themed
- Travel/visitor-focused content (when discovery is the goal)

## When NOT to use this style

- Loud party promos → use Bold Cantina Energy
- Late-night / dive-feel promos → use Street Taco Grit
- Family-oriented / kids brunch → use Family Fiesta
- Mezzanine evening events → use Premium Cocktail Lounge

## References

- Bon Appétit magazine layouts
- Eater editorial photography

## Anti-references

- NOT cluttered
- NOT corporate-stock — Ramsey's vocab specifically bans "vibrant atmosphere"
- NOT precious / Kinfolk-heavy
- NOT colorful — restraint is the rule
