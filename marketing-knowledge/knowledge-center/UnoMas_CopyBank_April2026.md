## HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

This is the master copy reference for all Uno Más marketing channels. Every piece of copy here is on-brand, ready to use, and built against the current menus (March 2026).

- **Lunch and Dinner posts are intentionally different in register.** Do not mix the tones.
- **Fill in placeholders** — anything in `[brackets]` needs a specific detail before use.
- **Email subject lines** are standalone — mix and match with any body template.
- **SMS messages** are TCPA-compliant and include opt-out language. Do not remove it. Stay under 160 characters.
- **Review responses** — always fill in `[Name]` and `[specific item]` before posting.
- **Hashtags** are provided per post. Use them as written unless there's a specific reason not to.
- **Never lead with price** in dinner content. The experience justifies itself.
- **Promotions** are campaign-based and intentional — price and deals language does not belong in standard brand content.

### Voice Pillars

**Pillar 1 — Self-Aware Confidence**
We know the food is excellent. We know the room is the best in Spokane. We don't need to announce it loudly. Confidence in this voice sounds quiet, specific, and certain. Price confidence lives here too: we never apologize, explain, or justify what things cost.

- ✅ "Carne Asada done right. That's it. That's the post."
- ✅ "Book the table. You'll thank yourself."
- ❌ "The BEST Mexican food in Spokane!!!"
- ❌ "You won't be disappointed!"

**Pillar 2 — Relaxed & Human**
Still conversational, still shareable. The humor is slower and smarter. Think Tom Segura landing a line for most content — Bert Kreischer energy is the gear for lunch, casual social, and high-energy moments only. Warm, never stiff. Occasionally funny in exactly the right place. The WTF menu copy is the internal benchmark: confident enough to describe something unusual without over-explaining.

- ✅ "Birria Tacos. Order these first. Thank us later."
- ✅ "Old school technique. Serious results."
- ❌ "GET HERE BEFORE YOUR FRIENDS DO 🚨🚨"
- ❌ Forced urgency, all-caps hype, flash-sale energy

**Pillar 3 — You're Somewhere Else Now**
The experience is the product as much as the food is. Uno Más transports people. When you walk in, Spokane recedes. Copy about the space, the occasion, or the vibe should describe the *feeling* — never generic hospitality language.

- ✅ "Inside. Feels like outside. Feels like somewhere else entirely."
- ✅ "The kind of room that makes you forget you have to work tomorrow."
- ❌ "A warm and inviting atmosphere perfect for any occasion."
- ❌ "Great for date night, family dinners, or corporate events!"

### Daypart Voice Rules

**LUNCH (11am–5pm)**
- Register: Casual, energetic, full personality on
- Humor dial: 7–8/10
- Emojis: 3–5 on social
- Lead with: Menu items, familiar items, the room's accessibility
- Taco language: Street taco and quarter-pound references are both appropriate
- Pricing: Only in promo campaign context — not standard content

**DINNER (5pm–Close)**
- Register: Confident, considered, experience-forward. Still human — never stiff.
- Humor dial: 4–5/10 — precise, not constant
- Emojis: 1–2 max on social. Zero on printed menus.
- Lead with: The experience, the plates, the occasion, the craft
- Taco language: Use sparingly if at all — lead with dinner plates and cocktails
- Pricing: Never in standard content. Let the experience justify itself.
- CTA: Every dinner post should have a path to booking a reservation
