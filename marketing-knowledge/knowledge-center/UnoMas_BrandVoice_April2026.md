**Version:** 2.1 — April 2026
**Owner:** Ramsey
**Applies To:** All marketing content — social, email, SMS, website, review responses, paid ads
**Does NOT Apply To:** Customer support issues, crisis communication, legal/HR/compliance
**Brand:** Uno Más Tacos & Tequila
**Location:** 2020 N Monroe St, Suite C — Spokane, WA 99205

## 01. WHO WE ARE

### The One-Liner
> Uno Más is a modern Mexican restaurant and tequila bar in Spokane where the food is serious, the atmosphere is alive, and the only thing we take lightly is ourselves.

### What We Are
Uno Más is a full-service Latin-inspired restaurant, cantina, and tequila bar at 2020 N Monroe in Spokane. We run a lunch program and an elevated dinner program. We have a full craft cocktail menu. We have The Mezzanine — a speakeasy and private event space upstairs. We are a gathering place. We are a destination.

We started as a taco shop. We grew into something more. Tacos are something we do exceptionally well — they are not the entirety of what we are.

### What We Are Not
- A taco shop (in brand descriptions — we've outgrown this)
- A fast-casual restaurant
- A chain
- A sports bar
- A family-friendly destination (21+ after 9pm)
- Fine dining in the traditional sense
- Pretentious about any of it

### The Name
**Uno Más** means one more. One more taco. One more margarita. One more reason to stay. The name is the brand. "Tacos & Tequila" is a descriptor that came with the name — honest but limited. It lives in the logo. It doesn't need to carry every piece of copy. Let the name do the work.

## 02. THE EXPERIENCE IN WORDS

This is one of the most underused assets in Uno Más marketing. The space is extraordinary and almost no one describes it well. These are the approved ways to talk about it.

### The Cantina Floor
- Inside. Feels like outside. Feels like somewhere else entirely.
- You feel like you're in a courtyard off a side street in Mexico — inside, but it doesn't feel like it.
- Dim enough to feel like evening. Open enough to feel alive.
- The kind of room that makes you forget you have to work tomorrow.
- The room does most of the work.

### The Mezzanine
- Upstairs, it gets quieter. Better.
- What happens when a speakeasy and a rooftop bar decide to share a space.
- Private. Moody. The kind of room where whatever you're celebrating actually feels celebrated.

### Full Experience Lines
- Uno Más is the kind of place you walk into for tacos and walk out having had a night.
- The kind of dinner you'll still be talking about Sunday.
- Gather your people. We'll handle the rest.

### What We Never Say About the Space
- ❌ "Warm and inviting atmosphere"
- ❌ "Perfect for any occasion"
- ❌ "Great for date night, family dinners, or corporate events"
- ❌ "Vibrant" (overused hospitality filler)
- ❌ Any language that could describe any restaurant anywhere

## 03. THE AUDIENCE

### Primary
Adults 25–45. Spokane locals and transplants who know the difference between good food and great food. Social diners. Cocktail drinkers. The person in the group chat who knows where to go. They value experience over deals. They're choosing Uno Más because it's worth it — not because it's cheap.

### Secondary
- Travelers and visitors to Spokane searching for the best dining experience in the city
- Event planners and groups booking The Mezzanine
- Weekend regulars and late-night diners (Fri & Sat DJ nights)
- Date-night diners planning ahead

### Who Is NOT Our Customer
Price shoppers. Coupon seekers. Anyone comparing us to Taco Bell, Del Taco, or $1 taco nights. That customer and those comparisons are not welcome in our copy — not even favorably. It anchors the wrong frame entirely.

### The Traveler Audience
We consistently hear from out-of-town guests that Uno Más is the best Mexican food they've had in a long time. This audience is real and valuable. They need:
- **Discovery:** Google Business optimized for "best Mexican Spokane," food travel language
- **Validation:** Reviews, social content showing the room + food together
- **Planning signal:** Copy that implies demand ("locals know to book ahead")
- **FOMO:** Language that makes it clear this place is always full

## 04. BRAND PERSONA

### The Human Behind the Voice
Uno Más is the friend who starts every story with "so we did a thing…" and somehow always knows where the party is. Confident without being cocky. Honest about the chaos. Deeply invested in you having a good time — and a great meal.

**Age:** 32–38
**Energy:** High, but grounded. Doesn't need to prove it.
**Humor:** Dry, specific, earned. Never try-hard.
**Social posture:** Peer first. The person at the table who says one perfectly timed thing and everyone laughs.

### Archetype
Jester + Everyman (with a dash of Rebel). Self-aware enough to acknowledge when things are imperfect. Confident enough not to apologize for what they charge.

### Comedian Energy Reference
**Tom Segura** — slow burn, precise, earns the laugh. Confident in the delivery. Doesn't need the room to agree immediately. *This is the default register — dinner content, The Mezzanine, brand-level copy.*

**Bert Kreischer** — high energy, self-aware, story-driven. Infectious and zero-filter but never mean. *This is the lunch/casual/social content register — when the volume should be up.*

The rule: know which register you're in before you write. Tom Segura for anything elevated. Bert Kreischer energy for casual, social, and lunch-hour content only.

## 05. VOICE PILLARS

### Pillar 1 — Self-Aware Confidence

We know the food is excellent. We know the room is the best in Spokane. We don't need to announce it loudly. Confidence in this voice sounds quiet, specific, and certain. It shows up in parenthetical asides, behind-the-scenes honesty, and confident specificity.

**What it sounds like:**
- "Carne Asada done right. That's it. That's the post."
- "Book the table. You'll thank yourself."
- "The one people come back for." *(three words, zero doubt)*
- "Old school technique. Serious results." *(no adjectives, all confidence)*

**What it does NOT mean:**
- Arrogance or bragging
- Fake humility ("we're just a little taco spot…")
- Apologizing for prices, policies, or popularity

**Price confidence lives here:** We never apologize, explain, or justify what things cost. The Surf & Turf is $47. The copy doesn't address that. The experience does.

---

### Pillar 2 — Relaxed & Human

Still conversational. Still shareable. The register has grown up with the audience — this isn't the screaming group chat anymore. It's the calm, confident person in the group chat who says something perfectly timed and everyone screenshots it.

Short, punchy sentences. Fragments are fine. Conversational rhythm. Text-message logic. The WTF menu copy is the internal benchmark for this voice — confident enough to describe something unusual without over-explaining: *"Looks crazy. You're going to have questions. The answer is just: try it."*

**What it sounds like:**
- "Birria Tacos. Order these first. Thank us later."
- "Tangy. Crunchy. Salty. You're welcome."
- "The Espresso Margarita is either dessert or a decision. Maybe both."

**What it does NOT mean:**
- Teen slang or forced memes
- Trying too hard to be viral
- All-caps energy and exclamation stacking
- Flash-sale urgency language

---

### Pillar 3 — You're Somewhere Else Now

The experience is the product as much as the food is. Uno Más transports people. When you walk in, Spokane recedes. The room feels like a courtyard in Mexico, a patio at a friend's place, a bar you stumbled into on a trip. The Mezzanine feels like a speakeasy someone told you about in a whisper.

This pillar shows up whenever we describe the space, the occasion, or the vibe — and it means we **never** describe the room in generic hospitality language. We describe the *feeling*.

**What it sounds like:**
- "Inside. Feels like outside. Feels like somewhere else entirely."
- "The kind of room that makes you forget you have to work tomorrow."
- "Upstairs, it gets quieter. Better."

**What it does NOT mean:**
- Overpromising
- Vague "vibe" language with no substance
- Describing the atmosphere without anchoring to the food and drinks that make it worth being there

## 06. TONE RANGE BY CONTEXT

| Situation | Tone | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Lunch / Tacos | Casual, energetic, playful | Full personality. Closest to original brand voice. |
| Dinner plates | Confident, considered, quiet | Let the food speak. Reservation CTA always. |
| Happy Hour / Cocktails | Flirty, self-aware | Lead with the drink, anchor to the experience |
| The Mezzanine | Cool, minimal, moody | Separate register — see Mezzanine section |
| Paid ads | Hook-driven, value-first | 60% clever max — clarity over cleverness in paid |
| Email / SMS | Friend texting good news | Personal, always include logistics |
| Social organic | Full personality | Most expressive channel — let it breathe |
| Review responses | Warm, direct, never corporate | See review response templates in Copy Bank |
| Customer issues | ❌ NOT this voice | Defer to direct, empathetic hospitality language |

## 07. DAYPART VOICE FRAMEWORK

### LUNCH VOICE (11am–5pm)

**Register:** Casual, energetic, direct. Full personality on.
**Humor dial:** 7–8/10
**Emojis:** 3–5 on social posts
**Copy length:** Punchy — 50–150 words for social
**Lead with:** Menu items, familiar proteins, the experience of being there at lunch
**Taco language:** Street taco and quarter-pound references both appropriate
**Price / deals:** Only in active promo campaign context — not standard content

Lunch is the more accessible face of Uno Más. It's still confident and on-brand — it's just louder and more direct. This is where the taco-shop energy lives, in its best form.

---

### DINNER VOICE (5pm–Close)

**Register:** Confident, considered, experience-forward. Still warm — never stiff.
**Humor dial:** 4–5/10 — precise and well-timed, not constant
**Emojis:** 1–2 max on social. Zero on printed dinner menus.
**Copy length:** Short and decisive — less is more
**Lead with:** The plates, the technique, the occasion, the experience of the room at night
**Taco language:** Use sparingly if at all — dinner content leads with plates and cocktails
**Price / deals:** Never. The experience justifies itself.
**Reservation CTA:** Every dinner post needs a path to booking

**The benchmark lines from our own menu — protect this voice:**
- "The one people come back for." *(Carne Asada)*
- "Old school technique. Serious results." *(Al Pastor)*
- "Bright, a little smoky, deeply good." *(Achiote Cilantro Shrimp)*
- "Everything included. Nothing held back. This is the move." *(Surf & Turf)*
- "Order these first. Thank us later." *(Birria Tacos)*

These five lines are the dinner voice defined. Short. Confident. Specific. No adjective stacking. The food speaks and the copy steps back.

**What dinner copy never does:**
- References cheap tacos, price comparisons, or value framing
- Uses deal language or discount signals
- Sounds like a chain restaurant ("Join us for an unforgettable dining experience!")
- Sounds like a hype account ("YOU NEED THIS IN YOUR LIFE 🔥🔥🔥")
- Over-explains the food with stacked adjectives
- Skips the reservation CTA

## 08. LANGUAGE RULES

### Sentence Structure
- Average sentence length: 8–15 words
- Fragments: YES — use them freely
- Questions: Sparingly — they work when they're genuine, not rhetorical filler
- Lists in copy: Short, three items max, punchy rhythm (e.g. "Tangy. Crunchy. Salty.")

### Required Phrases — By Context, Not Universal

| Phrase | When to Use |
|---|---|
| "Get a little lost at Uno Más" | Primary tagline — campaigns, social, menu footer, brand moments |
| "House-smoked" | Any content where smoked proteins are relevant — lunch or dinner |
| "Quarter-pound" | Lunch taco content, when size is specifically the story |
| "Street taco" | Direct menu item references — it's on the menu, it's fine to use |
| "Modern Mexican" | Google profiles, tourism content, long-form descriptions — not every post |
| "Latin-inspired" | Long-form brand descriptions, press materials — not a social refrain |
| "2020 N Monroe" | Include in most posts, especially paid — always in email and SMS |

### Always Include in Paid Content
- Location reference (2020 N Monroe or Monroe Ave)
- Clear CTA (Book a table / Come in / Reserve)
- Specific item or experience being promoted
