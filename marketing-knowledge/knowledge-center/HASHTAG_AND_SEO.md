## 1. HASHTAG STRATEGY OVERVIEW

### Philosophy
Hashtags serve two purposes: discoverability (reaching new audiences searching for content) and community signaling (showing we're a Spokane-first brand). Every hashtag we use should do at least one of those jobs.

We are not trying to reach the entire internet. We are trying to reach **Spokane and surrounding area** — people who live here, visit here, or are looking for things to do here. Hashtag selection should reflect that.

### Rules
- Never use a hashtag just to use one — each tag should have a clear reason
- Spokane-specific tags outperform generic national food tags for our audience
- Brand tags (#unomas, #getlittlelost) should be consistent — they build over time
- Rotate secondary tags to avoid hashtag fatigue with the algorithm
- 5–8 hashtags per Instagram post is the sweet spot — more is not better

## 5. HASHTAG USAGE RULES BY PLATFORM

### Instagram Feed Posts
- **Count:** 5–8 total
- **Placement:** End of caption OR first comment (both work — first comment keeps caption cleaner)
- **Mix:** Always 3–5 from Primary Set + 1–2 from content-specific set + brand tags where relevant
- **Never:** Use the same exact combination on every post — rotate to avoid algorithm penalty

### Instagram Stories
- Hashtags in Stories have limited discoverability impact — use 1–3 at most, or skip entirely
- Location tags (📍 sticker) matter more for Stories than hashtags

### Facebook
- Hashtags have minimal impact on Facebook discovery — use 2–3 max if at all
- Focus on the copy and the community, not the hashtag strategy

### TikTok (@unomastacosandtequila — Active)
- Keep tags minimal: 3–5 per post
- Mix trending food/discovery tags with Spokane-specific
- Starting set: `#foodtok` `#tacotok` `#spokanetiktok` `#spokaneeats` `#modernmexican`
- Trending audio matters more than hashtags on TikTok — prioritize sound strategy

## 6. HASHTAG PERFORMANCE NOTES

Based on archive analysis:

- `#spokane` (29 uses) and `#spokaneeats` / `#spokanefood` (27 uses each) are our most reliable tags — they consistently deliver Spokane-area impressions
- `#spokanetacos` (19 uses) is our most niche-specific tag — good for taco content where you want people actively searching for tacos in Spokane
- Brand tags (`#unomas`, `#getlittlelost`) build slowly but compound over time — use consistently
- High-volume generic tags (`#tacos`, `#foodporn`) can bring impressions but low conversion — pair with local tags to maintain relevance
- Promotion-specific tags perform best when tied to a clear, time-specific call to action

Sunday brunch launching Mother's Day — Sundays 10am–4pm. The Mezzanine — our upstairs speakeasy — is available for private events. Three venues in one address: The Cantina, The Mezzanine, and The Patio. Walk-ins always welcome. No delivery — we're a dine-in experience. Get a little lost."

### Categories to Maintain
- Primary: Mexican restaurant (closest available)
- Secondary: Bar, Taco restaurant, Latin American restaurant, Event venue

### Posts on Google Business
- Update weekly with current offers, hours changes, or new menu items
- Use SEO keywords naturally in post copy
- Always include a CTA (Reserve, Learn More, Call Now)

### Review Response SEO Note
Responding to Google reviews that include keywords (e.g., "best tacos in Spokane") helps reinforce those keyword associations. When responding, naturally echo relevant terms from their review.

## 9. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC SEO NOTES

### Instagram
- The account handle `@unomastacoshop` should appear consistently across all bio links
- Bio should include: category (modern Mexican restaurant & tequila bar), location (North Monroe, Spokane), and one CTA (reservations link or menu link)
- Instagram is now a search platform — captions with natural keyword language ("modern Mexican in Spokane," "Spokane speakeasy," "dinner Spokane") can surface in search

### Website (unomastacoshop.com)
Key SEO pages to prioritize:
- Homepage — lead with "Spokane's Latin-inspired taco shop and tequila bar on North Monroe"
- Menu page — item names as H2/H3 headers, descriptions include key terms naturally
- Events/Mezzanine page — target "private event venue Spokane," "private dining Spokane"
- About page — brand story with local Spokane references builds geographic relevance

### Yelp
- Keep business description updated with current menu highlights and SEO terms
