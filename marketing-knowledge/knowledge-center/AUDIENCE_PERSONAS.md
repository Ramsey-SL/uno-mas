## 2. PERSONA 1 — THE SOCIAL DINER (PRIMARY)

### Who They Are
Spokane locals and transplants, ages 25–45, who choose where to eat based on the full picture: is this place alive, is the food actually good, will it make a good story? They are the "where should we go tonight?" person in their friend group. When they find a spot they love, they bring everyone they know.

### What Drives Them
- FOMO — they don't want to miss what everyone else is doing
- Group experience — dining is a social act, not a solo one
- Local pride — Spokane-specific references land hard
- Vibe first — they assess the atmosphere before they look at the menu
- Shareable moments — if it's worth going, it's worth posting

### What They Don't Care About
- Lengthy menu descriptions
- Corporate story-telling
- Being sold to
- Price (primarily) — they'll spend if the experience justifies it

### How They Talk
Casual, group-chat energy. "Have you been to Uno Más yet?" "We should go Thursday." "Ok but the patio though." They share things by tagging people, not writing reviews.

### What Moves Them to Action
- A post that makes them feel like they're missing something
- A friend tagging them
- A specific detail that triggers curiosity ("Wait, there's a speakeasy upstairs?")
- A seasonal or time-limited reason to go NOW

### Voice Calibration for This Persona
Group Chat Energy is the dominant pillar. Self-Aware Confidence seals the deal. Write like you're the friend texting them about a spot, not a brand promoting to a customer.

### Best Content for This Persona
- Atmosphere and experience posts
- Patio season content
- WTF Menu drops ("you need to try this")
- The Mezzanine tease content
- Giveaways with simple entry mechanics
- Behind-the-scenes moments

## 3. PERSONA 2 — THE EVENT PLANNER

### Who They Are
Ages 28–50. Planning a birthday, bachelorette, corporate team outing, rehearsal dinner, or client event. They have a budget, a headcount, and a goal: make it memorable without it being stressful. They need a venue that handles the experience so they look like a hero.

### What Drives Them
- A space that feels special but isn't pretentious
- Clear pricing and package information upfront
- Flexibility to customize
- A venue that can handle the group size
- Knowing exactly what they're getting

### What They Don't Care About
- Being wowed by copy — they need information
- Vague "unforgettable experiences" language
- Having to hunt for logistics

### How They Find Us
- Instagram (Mezzanine-specific content)
- Direct referral from someone who's been
- Google search for private event venues in Spokane
- DM inquiry after seeing event-focused content

### What Moves Them to Action
- A clear package name and price range
- Photos of the Mezzanine space
- Easy inquiry path (DM, email, or form link)
- Social proof — "we hosted X here and it was incredible"

### Voice Calibration for This Persona
Core Voice but dialed toward professional warmth. Less jokes, more confidence. Give them the information they need in a voice that still feels like Uno Más. The Mezzanine packages (The Essentials, The Fiesta, The Full Send) do a lot of the work — let the names and inclusions speak.

### Best Content for This Persona
- Mezzanine space photography and video
- Package callouts (even just "groups of 15–37, we have a room for you")
- "How to book" clarity in bio / website
- User-generated content from past events
- "What's included" breakdowns (Reels or Stories work well)

## 4. PERSONA 3 — THE WEEKEND REGULAR

### Who They Are
Ages 25–50. They've been to Uno Más multiple times. They have a usual order. They know the staff names or at least recognize faces. Uno Más is part of their Spokane routine — Friday nights, Sunday brunch, the go-to when someone asks "where should we eat?"

### What Drives Them
- Feeling recognized and appreciated
- Consistency in quality and experience
- Being in on the inside — knowing things others don't (new menu items, what's coming)
- The loyalty program (Uno Mas Rewards: The Cantina Club) — they care about being rewarded

### What They Don't Care About
- Discovery content — they already know us
- Basic "come try us!" energy
- Repeating information they already have

### How to Reach Them
- Email (they've opted in)
- SMS (they've opted in)
- Instagram Stories (they're already following)

### What Moves Them to Action
- An exclusive early look at something new
- A loyalty perk or reward nudge
- A seasonal item or limited offering that creates urgency
- Feeling like the brand remembers them

### Voice Calibration for This Persona
This is where inside-joke language, callbacks, and "you already know" phrasing works well. They're not a prospect — they're part of the community. Write for them as such.

### Best Content for This Persona
- Email campaigns: new items, seasonal drops, loyalty program updates
- SMS: time-sensitive nudges ("patio's open tonight, just saying")
- Early access or "we're doing a thing" teasers
- WTF Menu monthly drops — they're the most likely to try it
- Behind-the-scenes content on Stories

## 5. PERSONA 4 — THE COCKTAIL & TEQUILA ENTHUSIAST

### Who They Are
Ages 28–45. Cares as much about what's in their glass as what's on their plate. They have opinions about tequila — they know the difference between blanco, reposado, and añejo. They appreciate a cocktail program that takes its drinks seriously without being insufferable about it. The Espresso Margarita and the tequila bar identity are the draws.

### What Drives Them
- A cocktail menu with personality and craft
- Tequila and agave spirits treated with respect
- A bar that can explain what they're drinking
- Rotating specials and seasonal cocktail drops as a compelling reason to show up

### What They Don't Care About
- "Refreshing beverages" generic language
- Cocktail descriptions that don't tell them what's in the drink
- Vibe without substance on the drinks side

### How to Reach Them
- Instagram (cocktail feature content, happy hour)
- In-venue — the menu itself and server recommendations

### What Moves Them to Action
- Named cocktails with specific ingredient callouts
- "Tequila bar" identity content
- Rotating specials and seasonal cocktail callouts
- The Indaba x Espresso Margarita crossover (local partnership angle)

### Voice Calibration for This Persona
Confident and specific. Name the spirit, name the ingredient, name the cocktail. No fluff. Slightly flirty — this persona appreciates a cocktail with personality that matches the drink's personality.

### Best Content for This Persona
- Cocktail spotlight posts — specific ingredients, no generic language
- Rotating special and seasonal cocktail content
- Tequila bar identity posts — own this lane in Spokane
- Seasonal / rotating frozen margarita flavor announcements
- Behind-the-bar content (Stories)

## 6. PERSONA 5 — THE FOOD EXPLORER

### Who They Are
Ages 22–40. Discovery-driven. They want to try things they haven't had before. They're the person at the table who orders the thing no one else is sure about, and then makes everyone taste it. The WTF Menu was made for them. The Tostilocos, the Tijuana Flatbread, the Birria — these are their entry points.

### What Drives Them
- Novelty and surprise
- Food that's hard to find anywhere else in Spokane
- The story behind what they're eating
- Being the person who "found" a spot before everyone else

### What Moves Them to Action
- WTF Menu drops — monthly newness is a built-in hook
- Menu item features that lead with the unexpected detail
- Instagram content that answers "wait, what IS that?"
- Tostilocos, Birria, Tijuana Flatbread content specifically

### Voice Calibration for This Persona
WTF Menu energy is native to this persona. The controlled chaos pillar is most active here. Lead with the unexpected, commit to the bit, let them be curious.

### Best Content for This Persona
- WTF Menu monthly drop announcements
- "What is this?" / "Try it" style content
- Short Reels of unexpected items being made or served
- "New Flavors. Made With Curiosity." framing — it's already in the brand

## 6B. PERSONA 6 — THE TRAVELER (SECONDARY, GROWING)

### Who They Are
Visitors to Spokane — business travelers, tourists, people passing through. Ages 25–55. They ask the concierge, search Google, or listen to locals. When they find us, they consistently report Uno Más as the best Mexican food they've had in a long time. They have a high likelihood of becoming online advocates (Google reviews, social posts) and spreading word of mouth post-visit.

### What Drives Them
- Wanting the "real" local experience, not a chain
- Social proof — reviews, photos, word of mouth
- Easy discovery (Google search, TripAdvisor, hotel recommendations)
- A memorable meal they'll talk about when they get home

### What Moves Them to Action
- Strong Google reviews and rating
- Clear search presence ("best restaurant Spokane," "modern Mexican Spokane")
- A great photo in a social post that stops them scrolling
- The story — converted garage, speakeasy upstairs, local landmark

### Voice Calibration
Same energy as Social Diner — they respond to "you found something." Frame Uno Más as the spot locals love, not a tourist stop. That distinction matters.

### Best Content for This Persona
- Google Business Profile — keep current, photos recent, description optimized
- Review responses — travelers read these; warm, personal responses build trust
- Instagram presence with location tags (Spokane, North Monroe)
- Any content that leads with the atmosphere and discovery angle

## 7. AUDIENCE-WIDE TRUTHS

These are true regardless of which persona you're writing for:

- **They are informed.** They know good food and good drinks. Don't oversell — they'll notice.
- **They choose vibe + quality together.** Neither alone is enough.
- **They are not primarily price-driven.** Price sensitivity is not a primary motivator for our core audience. Lead with experience.
- **They are local first.** Spokane pride resonates. National trends matter less than Monroe Street.
- **They share things they love.** Content that earns a share or a tag is the highest-performing content.
- **They don't want to feel marketed to.** Write like you're telling them something, not selling them something.

## 8. WHO WE ARE NOT WRITING FOR

Understanding who is NOT our audience helps keep content focused:

- ❌ Extreme bargain hunters whose primary question is "what's the cheapest thing?"
- ❌ People who want fine dining formality — we're casual confidence, not white tablecloth
- ❌ People looking for delivery — we are dine-in only, always
- ❌ Kids-first family dining — we're welcoming to families but 21+ after 9pm is core to the Mezzanine identity
- ❌ People who need to be convinced that Latin-inspired food is worthwhile — our audience already knows
