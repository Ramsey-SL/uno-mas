## 4. PROVEN ENGAGEMENT TRIGGERS

These patterns consistently increase engagement — use them intentionally, not as a checklist.

### Language Triggers
- **"Get a little lost" language:** 2x average engagement vs. posts without it
- **"So we did a thing…" opener:** Generates comments asking what happened
- **Specific prices in promotional context:** Outperform vague "specials" language every time
- **Questions:** Drive 3x more comments than statements — but use sparingly, max one per post
- **Direct social prompts** ("send this to," "tag someone who"): Multiply reach organically

### Structural Triggers
- **Posts with specific details** (prices, times, item names): Consistently outperform vague equivalents
- **Novelty announcements** ("NEW.[THING]"): Strong discovery-phase performance
- **Scene-setting openers** (no logo, no brand name — just a visual detail): Stop the scroll
- **Short posts** (under 150 characters in the visible portion): Higher engagement than long-form

### Format Triggers
- **Reels outperform static posts** in reach — prioritize video when possible
- **Behind-the-scenes content** (raw, unpolished) outperforms produced content in engagement rate
- **UGC-style posts** (user-generated or written to feel that way) perform like discovery content

## 5. CONTENT CATEGORY PERFORMANCE

Based on categorized post analysis:

| Category | Post Count (Archive) | Engagement Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Community / Local | 105 | Consistent mid-tier — loyal audience |
| Menu Highlights | 64 | Peaks on novelty items, flat on repeats |
| Promotions | 59 | Strong with specific prices, weak when vague |
| Events | 46 | Strong when 48hr advance, weak same-day |
| Engagement / Giveaways | 25 | High comment volume, moderate reach |

### Observations
- **Community posts** are the most consistent — they never bomb but rarely go viral. They maintain the base.
- **Menu posts** need a specific angle to perform above average. "Here's our burrito" alone doesn't cut it — "Here's the Notorious P.I.G. and here's why" does.
- **Promotional posts** live and die by specificity. "$10 for 2 tacos and a side" outperforms "lunch deals" every time.
- **Events** need lead time. 48-hour minimum advance posting for weekend events. Same-day posts for events underperform significantly.

|---|---|---|---|
| Response / Action Rate | 12%+ | 5–11% | <5% |
| Opt-Out Rate | <2% | 2–4% | >4% |

### SMS Performance Patterns
- **Time-sensitive messages** ("patio is open tonight") outperform general promotional messages
- **Specificity** ("Dinner from 5pm — book now" or "patio is live tonight") outperforms vague nudges
- **Frequency matters:** More than once a week drives opt-outs. Aim for 2–4x/month.
- **Personalization** (name or loyalty reference) improves action rate

## 9. WHAT CONSISTENTLY UNDERPERFORMS

Avoid these patterns — they have a documented track record of weak performance.

| Pattern | Why It Underperforms |
|---|---|
| Generic "come in and eat with us" posts | No hook, no specificity, no reason to act |
| Vague "specials available" without prices | Audience doesn't act on what they can't evaluate |
| Same-day event announcements | No lead time means no decision-making window |
| Posts with only product shots, no copy | Copy carries the voice — silent posts lose brand identity |
| Repetitive promotional content with no variation | Audience tunes out, engagement drops week over week |
| Corporate-sounding captions | Immediately breaks brand trust — "salesperson" energy detected |
| Overloaded posts trying to say too many things | One message per post — multiple messages = zero messages |

## 11. AGENT DECISION RULES (BUILT FROM DATA)

These rules are derived directly from performance patterns above. Apply them when making content decisions.

**Rule 1:** Lead with experience or discovery, not the offer. Save the specifics for the close.

**Rule 2:** Always include at least one specific detail — a price (when promotional), an item name, a time. Vague posts underperform.

**Rule 3:** For food features, let the ingredient list tell the story. Don't editorialize with food clichés.

**Rule 4:** For event content, post at minimum 48 hours in advance. Same-day posts don't move people.

**Rule 5:** One clear message per post. Two messages dilutes both.

**Rule 6:** Questions drive comments but use sparingly — one question max, and only if it earns its place.

**Rule 7:** Reels outperform static for reach. When video assets exist, prioritize Reel format.

**Rule 8:** Behind-the-scenes and "coming soon" content consistently outperforms polished produced content for engagement rate. Authenticity reads.

**Rule 9:** Emoji should add something, not pad. When in doubt, leave it out.

**Rule 10:** For email, test both emoji and no-emoji subject lines before establishing a default. We don't have enough A/B data yet to make a rule.
