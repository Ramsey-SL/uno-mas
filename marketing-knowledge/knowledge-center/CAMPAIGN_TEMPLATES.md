## 2. SOCIAL MEDIA TEMPLATES

---

### 2A. THE FOOD STORY POST
**When to use:** Featuring a specific menu item — not a promotion, just making someone hungry and curious.
**Persona target:** Social Diner, Food Explorer
**Emoji:** Optional — only if it adds something

**Template:**
```
[Item name — let the name do the work. Don't explain it yet.]
[One specific detail that makes it real — a key ingredient, a contrast, something unexpected]
[One line that earns the feeling without overselling it]
[Optional soft CTA — "on the menu now" or "you know where to find us"]
```

**Example (Burritos):**
```
The Notorious P.I.G.
House smoked pork, pickled onion, salsa verde, guacamole.
It's a burrito. It's also a commitment. We respect both.
On the menu. Come hungry.
```

**Example (Birria):**
```
Birria Tacos.
Braised chuck, melted Monterey Jack, consomé for dipping.
The kind of thing you think about on the drive home.
```

**Watch out for:** Don't add price unless it's a promotional context. Don't describe it as "mouthwatering" or "delicious" — let the ingredients tell the story.

---

### 2B. THE COCKTAIL FEATURE POST
**When to use:** Spotlighting a specific cocktail or pushing the tequila bar identity.
**Persona target:** Cocktail Enthusiast, Social Diner
**Emoji:** 🍹 if used — one max

**Template:**
```
[Cocktail name].
[Key ingredients — specific, no filler words]
[One line about what it does / why it's worth ordering]
[Optional: "Happy Hour 3–5pm" if relevant]
```

**Example:**
```
Espresso Margarita.
Tequila blanco, Indaba cold brew, Baileys, agave.
The cocktail that finally ends the coffee vs. drinks debate.
Happy Hour 3–5pm. 🍹
```

**Example (no emoji):**
```
The Jamaica Spritz.
Tequila, jamaica syrup, lime, St. Germain, agave, champagne.
It shows up. Every time.
```

**Watch out for:** Never say "refreshing" or "crafted with care." Name the spirit, name the ingredients, trust the menu.

---

### 2C. THE ATMOSPHERE / EXPERIENCE POST
**When to use:** Not promoting a specific item or deal — selling the feeling of being here.
**Persona target:** Social Diner, Weekend Regular
**Emoji:** Optional. If used, ☀️ for patio, 🔥 or 🪩 for Mezzanine/evening

**Template:**
```
[Scene-setting detail — one specific, visual thing about the space]
[What it feels like to be there]
[The invitation — soft, not pushy]
```

**Example (Main Floor):**
```
An old mechanic's garage on Monroe Street.
Now it looks like this.
Get a little lost at Uno Más.
```

**Example (Evening):**
```
The kind of Tuesday you didn't know you needed.
Come find us.
```

**Example (Mezzanine):**
```
Fireplace. Leather. A bar upstairs you didn't know was there.
Now you do.
```

**Watch out for:** Resist the urge to list features. One detail is more evocative than five. Don't mention location unless it's genuinely part of the story.

---

### 2D. THE PATIO POST
**When to use:** Patio season — announcing it's open, pushing traffic on good weather days.
**Persona target:** Social Diner (primary)
**Emoji:** ☀️ fits here

**Core Voice Template:**
```
[Simple declaration — "The patio is open."]
[One reason it matters right now — weather, season, vibe]
[Optional CTA]
```

**Party Promoter Template:**
```
[Bold declaration about being outside]
[Why staying inside is the wrong choice right now]
[Come get here energy]
```

**Core Voice Example:**
```
The patio is open. ☀️
Margaritas. Monroe Street. Good company.
See you out there.
```

**Party Promoter Example:**
```
The patio is open and I genuinely don't understand why you're still inside.
Get out here. Bring someone. We'll handle the rest.
```

---

### 2E. THE WTF MENU DROP
**When to use:** Monthly WTF Menu launch or spotlight on an experimental item.
**Persona target:** Food Explorer, Social Diner
**Emoji:** Only if the item calls for it (🔥 for wings, 🌮 for tacos)

**Template:**
```
[Item name — all caps or bold energy]
[The item's own logic — use the WTF Menu copy as your guide]
[Commit to the bit — one line that leans into the absurdity]
[Price only if it's unusually compelling]
```

**Example (Tostilocos):**
```
TOSTILOCOS.
Salsa verde chips, chamoy, cucumber, jicama, tajin, tamarindo candy, Japanese peanuts.
Looks crazy. You're going to have questions. The answer is: try it.
```

**Example (Lula Wings):**
```
Lula Wings.
House-made Cholula sauce, crema, cilantro, cotija. 🔥
Sorry in advance to every wing spot you've ever loved.
8 wings, $12. 30 wings if you're serious.
```

**Watch out for:** Don't sanitize the WTF Menu. The chaos IS the appeal. The brand's own copy for these items is the gold standard — use it.

---

### 2F. THE DISCOVERY / HYPE POST
**When to use:** Introducing Uno Más to new audiences, boosting reach, driving follows.
**Persona target:** Social Diner (new)
**Emoji:** 2–3 feel right here — this is a higher-energy format

**Template:**
```
[Bold claim about what this place is]
[The specific detail that proves it — not generic]
[The invitation to tag or share]
[Location only if needed for a discovery context]
```

**Example:**
```
Spokane's tequila bar. Spokane's taco shop. Both.
Quarter-pound tacos, house smoked meats, and a room upstairs you didn't know existed.
Tag someone who needs to know about this. 🌮🍹
```

### 2G. THE COMMUNITY / LOCAL LOVE POST
**When to use:** Connecting with Spokane, celebrating partnerships, neighborhood pride.
**Persona target:** Weekend Regular, Social Diner
**Emoji:** 💙 fits here

**Template:**
```
[Acknowledgment of something local / community-specific]
[How it connects to Uno Más]
[Invitation that feels genuine, not promotional]
```

**Example (Indaba Partnership):**

Local cold brew, tequila blanco, Baileys, agave.

### 2H. THE MEZZANINE EVENT / PRIVATE EVENT POST
**When to use:** Promoting private event availability or a specific Mezzanine moment.
**Persona target:** Event Planner, Social Diner

**Template:**
```
[What the space is — one evocative detail, not a list]
[Who it's for / what it's for]
[Clear CTA — DM us, link in bio, email to book]
```

**Example:**
```
There's an upstairs at Uno Más.
Fireplace, private bar, room for your whole group.

DM us to book. Or find the link in bio.

## 3. EMAIL CAMPAIGN TEMPLATES

---

### 3A. PROMOTIONAL EMAIL — HAPPY HOUR / SPECIAL
**When to use:** Time-sensitive promotional push.
**Subject line options:** Test both emoji and no-emoji versions.

```
SUBJECT (with emoji test): Happy Hour starts at 3. 🍹
SUBJECT (no emoji test): Happy Hour starts at 3.
PREVIEW: Every day. The details are below.

---

Hey [first name or "Taco Fam"],

Happy Hour runs 3–5pm every day we're open.

[One specific cocktail or deal callout if applicable]

[Clear CTA button: "Come In Today" / "Reserve a Spot"]

We're at [address if new subscriber list] / See you soon.

— [Name or Uno Más]
```

---

### 3B. NEW ITEM ANNOUNCEMENT EMAIL
**When to use:** WTF Menu drop, new menu addition, seasonal item launch.

```
SUBJECT (test A): So we did a thing.
SUBJECT (test B): New on the menu. You need to try it.
PREVIEW: [Specific item name + one-line tease]

---

[Opening — energetic, not formal]

[Item name + description in brand voice — pull from WTF Menu copy if applicable]

[Why now / how long it's available — urgency without pressure]

[CTA: "See the Full Menu" / "Come Try It"]

— [Name or Uno Más]
```

---

### 3C. SEASONAL / PATIO LAUNCH EMAIL
**When to use:** Patio opening, seasonal menu drop, "something has changed" moment.

```
SUBJECT (test A): The patio is open.
SUBJECT (test B): We did a thing. Come see.
PREVIEW: [Specific seasonal detail]

---

[Opening that sets the scene — not "we are excited to announce"]

[What's new / what's open / what to expect]

[One specific reason to come now]

[CTA: "Get Out Here" / "Reserve Your Spot"]

— [Name or Uno Más]
```

---

### 3D. LOYALTY / COMMUNITY EMAIL
**When to use:** Rewarding existing customers, Uno Mas Rewards: The Cantina Club loyalty nudge, "thank you for being a regular" moment.

```
SUBJECT (test A): You've been here a while. We noticed.
SUBJECT (test B): This one's for the regulars.
PREVIEW: [Specific reward or inside detail]

---

[Acknowledge them — not generically, but specifically]

[The offer, reward, or exclusive info]

[CTA: "Claim Your Reward" / "See What's New"]

— [Name or Uno Más]
```

## 4. SMS TEMPLATES

**Hard rules:** 160 characters max including opt-out. 1–2 emojis max. Always end with "Reply STOP to opt out." Include price only if the message is deal-driven.

---

## 5. REVIEW RESPONSE TEMPLATES

**Rules:** Under 100 words. First name always. Reference something specific. Sign with a real name. No corporate language.

---

### 5A. POSITIVE REVIEW RESPONSE
```
[First name], [genuine reaction — not "thank you so much!"].
[Reference the specific thing they mentioned — item, person, moment].
[Warm close — invite them back or express something real].
— [Team member name]
```

**Example:**
```
Maria, this made our whole week.
The Espresso Margarita does that to people — we've seen it happen.
Come back soon. We'll make sure it's just as good.
— Karissa
```

---

### 5B. NEGATIVE REVIEW RESPONSE
```
[First name], [acknowledge their experience without being defensive or using corporate language].
[Specific thing you want to make right — not generic "we'll do better"].
[Direct path to resolution — contact info or invitation to come back].
— [Team member name]
```

**Example:**
```
James, that's not the experience we want anyone to have.
We'd love to make it right — reach out to us directly at [phone/email] and we'll take care of you.
— Ramsey
```

---

### 5C. NEUTRAL / MIXED REVIEW RESPONSE
```
[First name], [acknowledge what they enjoyed].
[Address the concern honestly — one sentence, no defensiveness].
[Invitation back with a specific reason].
— [Team member name]
```

## 6. SEASONAL CAMPAIGN FRAMEWORKS

### Summer — Patio Season
**Theme:** The outside is the experience. Monroe Street. Warm nights. Margaritas.
**Primary content:** Patio open announcements, outdoor atmosphere, evening vibes
**Tone:** Party Promoter for patio launch, Core Voice for regular season content
**Key phrases:** "The patio is open" / "Get outside" / "Margaritas and Monroe Street"
**Hashtags:** Add `#spokanepatio` `#patioszn` to rotation

### Fall — The Return Inside
**Theme:** The cantina pulls you back in. Warm, comfortable, an escape from the grey.
**Primary content:** Interior atmosphere, cozy evening content, cocktail warmth
**Tone:** Core Voice — warm, slightly nostalgic
**Key phrases:** "Come back inside" / "Warm up with us" / "The cantina in fall"

### Winter — The Escape
**Theme:** Leave winter outside. The space transports you somewhere warmer.
**Primary content:** Mezzanine fireplace, interior warmth, late-night energy
**Tone:** Core Voice for warmth, Party Promoter for holiday event content
**Key phrases:** "Get a little lost" / "Leave winter at the door" / "The Mezzanine in December"

### Spring — Something New
**Theme:** New menu items, patio prep, the energy of a fresh season.
**Primary content:** Menu drops, patio tease ("coming soon"), WTF Menu spring edition
**Tone:** Party Promoter for new season launch, Core Voice for menu content

## 7. CONTENT CALENDAR FRAMEWORK

### Recommended Weekly Content Mix (Per Channel)

**Instagram Feed (5–7 posts/week):**
- 2–3 food/menu features
- 1–2 experience/atmosphere posts
- 1 cocktail or tequila bar feature
- 1 community or engagement post (question, share prompt)
- 1 promotional post (happy hour, current special)

**Instagram Stories (Daily if possible):**
- Behind-the-scenes moments
- Daily specials or happy hour reminder
- Polls / engagement (what should we add to the WTF Menu?)
- Event or patio teasers
- User-generated content reshares

**Email (1–2x/week):**
- 1 promotional (happy hour, seasonal item, specific deal)
- 1 content/community (new item story, brand moment, loyalty)

**SMS (2–4x/month):**
- Time-sensitive only — happy hour push, limited item alert, patio open
- Never more than once a week unless there's a genuine reason

### Monthly Content Calendar Rhythm

| Week | Focus |
|---|---|
| Week 1 | New WTF Menu drop + any seasonal launch |
| Week 2 | Food features + cocktail spotlight |
| Week 3 | Mezzanine / event push (mid-month visibility) |
| Week 4 | Community + engagement content + next month tease |

```
