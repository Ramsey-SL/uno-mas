# Customer Profile — Uno Más Tacos & Tequila

## Who They Are
- **Primary Customer:** Spokane locals and transplants, ages 25–45, who value experience over just a meal. Social diners. Cocktail drinkers. The person who answers "where should we go?" in the group chat.
- **Secondary Audiences:**
  - Corporate event planners and hosts booking The Mezzanine
  - Date-night couples seeking something impressive without full fine-dining commitment
  - Brunch crowd (Sundays starting May)
  - Late-night 21+ crowd
  - **Travelers and visitors to Spokane** — consistently report Uno Más as the best Mexican food they've had in a long time. Discover us via Google search, word of mouth, and local recommendations. Need: strong Google presence, social proof, and copy that signals we're worth planning around.
- **Decision-Maker:** Individuals, couples, friend groups, and "experience planners" — the one who curates the night out

## When They Come
**Primary occasions:**
- Weeknight dinner — spontaneous or planned, looking for somewhere worth it

## Switching Dynamics

- **Push** (frustrations with alternatives): Boredom with the same restaurant rotation. Disappointment from picking the wrong place. Tired of delivery fees for cold food. Fine dining feels stuffy and requires planning.
- **Pull** (what attracts them to Uno Más): The vibe. The food quality. The "I found something" feeling. The story to tell friends. Now: a dinner menu that surprises people.
- **Habit** (their current default): Familiar restaurants, cooking at home, delivery app convenience.
- **Anxiety** (what holds them back): "Is it worth the drive to Monroe?" "I've never heard of it." "Is the dinner menu worth $37–47?" "Will it feel like a real dinner or just a casual spot?"

**Primary lever for dinner campaign:** **Pull** — The dinner menu creates desire. Surf & Turf, a Raw Bar, the $129 Feast — this isn't what people expect, and that surprise is the hook.
