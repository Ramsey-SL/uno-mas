# Creative Brief — Dinner Hits Different Now

> From: Campaign Strategist
> To: Creative Director

> Date: March 21, 2026 (Revised — post-soft-launch)

---

## Strategic Context

Uno Más soft-launched a fully revamped dinner menu last week at their Monroe Street location in Spokane. Dinner plates ($24–$47), a Raw Bar with oysters and ceviche, and the Uno Más Feast ($129 for 2-3) — this is a major elevation from taco shop to dinner destination. Early guest feedback is strong. Now we're going full push starting March 28 to spread the word across Spokane. Budget is $2,500 total. Food photography is the star of every piece of creative. The brand voice is confident, playful, group-chat energy — never corporate, never precious.

---

## The Core Message

**Uno Más just became Spokane's most unexpected dinner destination — and the people who've already tried it can't shut up about it.**

## Audience

- **Who**: Spokane locals 25–45 who decide where the group goes for dinner. Within 10 miles of 2020 N Monroe.
- **Journey stage**: Consideration → Decision. They've maybe heard of Uno Más for tacos. They don't know about dinner yet.
- **Key belief to create**: "Uno Más isn't just a taco spot anymore. They're doing real dinner — and it's legit."
- **Psychological lever**: **Pull** — Desire + surprise. "I had no idea they had a $47 Surf & Turf and oysters."

---

## Tone for This Campaign

The Uno Más voice, turned up: confident, playful, self-aware, a little chaotic. This is "so we did a thing…" energy.

**DO**:
- Write like a text from a friend: "You need to come try this. Not kidding."
- Be specific: "Skirt steak + tiger prawns + cilantro lime rice" not "delicious new dishes"
- Use the brand's own language: "hit different," "your move," "get a little lost," "so we did a thing"
- Let the food photography do the heavy lifting — copy is short and punchy
- Use 3-5 emojis on social (👀🌮✨💙🍹)

**DON'T**:
- Say "street tacos" (ever)
- Say "authentic Mexican" or "mouthwatering" or "culinary journey"
- Stack adjectives ("fresh, flavorful, delicious, hearty")
- Sound corporate ("We're pleased to announce our new dinner offerings")
- Oversell — the food speaks. Just point people to it.
- Use "amazing" generically

---

## Proof Point to Lead With

**Soft launch social proof**: The menu has been live for a week. People have already tried it and love it. Lead with "the verdict is in" energy — not "come try something new and scary."

Example framing:
- "We quietly dropped a whole new dinner menu last week. The verdict? It hits different."
- "People have been asking if the Surf & Turf is real. It's real. It's $47. And it's the move."
