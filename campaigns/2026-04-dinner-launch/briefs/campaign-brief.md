## 2. Audience Brief

**Primary Segment**: Spokane locals ages 25–45 who are the "where should we go?" person in the group chat — within a 10-mile radius of 2020 N Monroe.

**Secondary Segment**: Existing Uno Más regulars who know the brand for tacos and drinks but haven't experienced the dinner menu yet.

**Journey Stage**: Consideration → Decision (awareness among regulars is partially built from soft launch; new diners need a reason to choose Uno Más for dinner over their usual spot)

**The job they're hiring this campaign to do**:
> "When I'm picking a spot for dinner this weekend, I want somewhere that's actually worth getting dressed for — good food, good drinks, good energy — so I can have a great night out without overthinking it."

**Key psychological lever**:
> **Pull** — Desire. The Surf & Turf, the Uno Más Feast, the Raw Bar — these aren't what people expect from "a taco shop." That surprise IS the hook. Create the "wait, Uno Más has THAT now?" reaction.

**The belief this campaign must create or reinforce**:
> "Uno Más isn't just tacos anymore. They're doing real dinner plates — and the people who've been are saying it hits different."

**Audience exclusions**:
- Fast food / delivery-only seekers
- Price-first decision-makers who won't pay $37 for Carne Asada
- Anyone outside reasonable driving distance to Monroe

---

## 3. Messaging Territory

**Core Message** (one sentence):
> Uno Más just became Spokane's most unexpected dinner destination — and the people who've already tried it can't shut up about it.

**Support Messages**:
- Surf & Turf for $47. A Raw Bar. The Uno Más Feast that feeds your whole crew for $129. This isn't what you expected from a taco shop. That's the point.
- The dinner menu soft-launched last week. The verdict: it hits different. Your move.
- Still got the quarter-pound tacos. Still got the Espresso Margarita. But now there's Carne Asada plates, Achiote Cilantro Shrimp, and oysters on the half shell.

**Proof Point to Lead With**:
> Real guest reactions from soft launch week. The menu is already validated — lean into "people are already talking" energy rather than "come try our new thing."

**Competitive Angle**:
> No other spot in Spokane gives you a $47 Surf & Turf, a speakeasy upstairs, live events, AND quarter-pound tacos — all under one converted-garage roof. Uno Más is playing a different game now.

**Tone for This Campaign**:
> Confident, playful, self-aware — the Uno Más voice turned up to 10. This is "so we did a thing…" energy. Not fine dining announcements. Not corporate reveals. Group-chat energy: "you HAVE to come try this."

**Angles to Avoid**:
- "We're pleased to announce" or any corporate language ❌
- "Street tacos" — never use this term ❌
- "Authentic Mexican" ❌
- "Mouthwatering" / "culinary journey" / adjective stacking ❌
- Discount/coupon as primary hook ❌
- Framing the old menu as broken ❌
- Apologetic or humble tone ❌
