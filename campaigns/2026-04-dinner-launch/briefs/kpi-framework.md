## Attribution

**Official reporting model**: Last-click via GA4 (or Squarespace Analytics if GA4 not fully configured)
**Reporting platform**: GA4 + platform-native dashboards for in-channel optimization
**In-platform optimization**: Meta and Google reported metrics for bid/targeting decisions only

**How we handle cross-channel deduplication**:
GA4 last-click is the source of truth for web-based conversions (menu page views, direction clicks). Platform metrics used for in-platform optimization only. Dinner covers tracked via Toast POS are attributed to the campaign as a whole — the true north star is "did total dinner traffic increase during the 30-day push window vs. the soft launch week baseline?"

**Important note**: At this budget level ($2,500), sophisticated multi-touch attribution isn't necessary or cost-effective. Focus on: did dinner covers go up? Did web traffic to the menu page increase? Did we reach 25K+ local people? Those three answers tell the story.

## Target-Setting Math

**Goal**: +30% dinner covers over soft launch week baseline
**Example** (hypothetical — replace with actual Toast data):
- If soft launch baseline: 100 dinner covers/week
- Target: 130 dinner covers/week = +30/week = +120 covers over 4-week campaign
- If average dinner check is $45: +120 covers × $45 = **$5,400 incremental revenue**
- Campaign cost: $2,500
- **Implied ROAS: 2.2x** (and that's just the measurable 30-day window — dinner habits formed now pay off for months)

**Assessment**: Achievable. The owned channels (email, SMS, organic social) carry major weight at this budget. Paid media amplifies — owned channels convert. And the soft launch proof makes every piece of creative more credible.
